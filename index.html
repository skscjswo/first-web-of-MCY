Frozen Shoulder란 무엇인가?

Adhesive Capsulitis is commonly referred to as “frozen shoulder.” It is a disorder characterized by pain and loss of motion or stiffness in the shoulder. The cause of frozen shoulder is not fully understood.
The process involves thickening and contracture of the capsule surrounding the shoulder joint. Frozen shoulder can develop after a shoulder is injured or immobilized for a period of time. Attempts to prevent frozen shoulder include early motion of the shoulder after it has been injured.
Frozen shoulder affects about two percent of the general population. It is most common in women between 40-70 years old. It is also associated with a number of medical problems including:

Diabetes

  • Hypothyroidism
  • Hyperthyroidism
  • Parkinson's Disease
  • Cardiac disease
